Skills

From Aethermancer Wiki
Jump to navigation Jump to search

Skills refer to abilities that monsters can learn and use in combat. Active Skills, known as Actions, are directly used during battles, while passive Skills, referred to as Traits, provide ongoing benefits or effects. Each monster species starts with two Actions and a Signature Trait. Additional Skills become available as the monster levels up. A monster can only learn Skills that match at least one of its Types. For Actions, the required spent Aether must also match at least one of the monster's assigned Elements.

Upon leveling up, the monster can learn either a new Trait or Action, depending on its level Traits on even levels and Actions on odd levels. When a monster learns a new Skill, three Skills are randomly selected from the monster's Skill pool, from which the player must choose one. Skill Rerolls, which can be obtained from meta-upgrades and loot drops, can be used to reroll the presented three options with three new ones. Lily can also replace an existing Trait or Action of a chosen monster with one of three available Skills, mirroring the process of learning a new Skill. Each monster can have up to four Actions (including the two they start with) and up to four Traits (excluding their Signature Traits). Once a cap is reached, the player can either grant the monster +5 Max Health or replace one of its current Skills with the newly selected Skill.


Maverick Skills

Maverick Skills are special Skills that combine two different Types, although not all Skills with two Types, such as Snow Charm, are considered Maverick. A monster can only learn a Maverick Skill if its Type matches at least one of the required Types and if there is a monster in the party with the other required Type. If a monster possesses both required Types, it can learn these Skills without needing another monster in the party. When a Maverick Skill is among the possible Skills a monster can learn, it has a 50% chance of appearing on the Skill selection screen, though only one Maverick Skill can appear at a time during Skill selection.

Maverick Skills are designed to enable synergies between different monster Types. Once learned, the Skill remains available even if there are no longer any monsters with the other required Type, unless the Skill is replaced.

Skill Selection System

Wyrmling selecting a new Trait

When a monster learns a new Skill, the selection is not entirely random; instead, it employs a weighted system. Each Skill begins with a weight of 1.0. When a monster learns a Skill, the weight of all other Skills of the same Type that the monster has not yet learned increases. For instance, if a monster has a Power Skill, it becomes more likely for the monster to learn additional Power Actions or Traits in the future.

The Skill's Type also influences the availability of Skills for other monsters in the party, albeit to a lesser extent. This mainly affects the availability of Maverick Skills or Skills for monsters of the same Type. For example, if a monster has a Sidekick Skill, the likelihood of a different Sidekick monster in the party learning a Sidekick Skill will slightly increase.

Signature Traits and starting Actions do not have any type assigned. Therefore, by default, the first generated Skills of the first monster each have a weight of 1.0. Some Skills also have certain acquisition requirements, such as a Skill that affects a particular buff or debuff may require the monster or an ally to possess a Skill capable of applying that buff or debuff. This prevents the appearance of "pointless" Skills during Skill selection.

Weight Values

Each Skill has a base weight of 1.0, which increases based on the Skills the monster and other monsters on the party has learned:

  • If the Skill is an Action with two Types (Maverick or non-Maverick) and the monster has both Types, adds 0.5 weight.
  • Each Skill on the monster that overlaps at least one Type adds 0.3 weight.
  • Each Skill on other monsters that overlaps at least one Type adds 0.075 weight.

The higher the weight of a Skill, the more likely it is to appear on the Skill selection screen. For example, the Skill weights for Wyrmling (Heal/Aether/Power) that has Aether Breath (Power+Aether) and Healing Brew (Heal/Aether) are:

  • Skills with Heal - 1.3 (1.0 + 0.3).
  • Traits with both Heal and Aether - 1.6 (1.0 + 0.3 + 0.3).
  • Actions with both Heal and Aether - 2.1 (1.0 + 0.5 + 0.3 + 0.3).
  • If there are two Power Skills on other allies, Skills with Power - 1.45 (1.0 + 0.3 + 0.075 + 0.075).
